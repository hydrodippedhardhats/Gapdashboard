What is GAP insurance, in plain English?
Cars lose value fast — but loan balances drop slowly. If your car gets totaled or stolen, your regular insurance only pays what the car is worth right now, not what you still owe the bank. That difference is called the "gap." Without GAP coverage, you'd have to pay that difference out of pocket — even though you no longer have the car.

The Bottom Line
Plain English recommendation for your exact loan
Don't buy the dealer's GAP insurance. You do have real risk — if your car were totaled today, you'd be on the hook for roughly — out of pocket. That's real money and worth protecting against.

But that risk disappears entirely around —, after which you always own more than you owe. The dealer wants to charge you $15/month for all 72 months ($1,080 total) to cover a risk that only exists for the first several months. That's a terrible deal.

What to do instead: Call your car insurance company (Progressive, GEICO, Erie, whoever you use) and ask to add "loan/lease payoff coverage" to your policy. It typically costs $20–40 for the whole year. Keep it for the first 12 months to be safe, then drop it. You'll have the exact same protection for roughly $30 instead of $1,080.
